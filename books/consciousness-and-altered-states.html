Overview

The book examines how states of meditation, contemplation, ecstatic absorption, and intensified interiority can alter one’s sense of time, self, meaning, and presence. It treats altered states not merely as anomalies, but as important data for understanding the range and flexibility of human awareness.

This volume is intended for readers interested in contemplative practice, spiritual experience, psychology, and the wider spectrum of consciousness beyond ordinary waking awareness.
